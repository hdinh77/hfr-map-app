Map Functions

  • Surface Current Vectors

    - To view the surface current vector at a particular region, simply click its location on the map, and a marker will pop up on the map at nearest data point.
    - This also opens up a display on the bottom left corner with information including the current vector's coordinates, u-v directional components, averaged timeframe, and resolution.

  • HFR Stations

    - HFR Stations are denoted on the map as green, white, yellow, and red markers.
    - The status of each station corresponds to the most recent time it was updated (more information in the "Legend" section).
    - Click on the station marker to view the station name, abbreviation, coordinates, network/affiliation, age, and a link to an external diagnostics page.
    - The station markers can also be toggled on and off in the "Stations and Oil Platforms" section of the Control Center.

  • Oil Platforms

    - Oil platforms (along the SoCal coast) are represented with the platform icon.
    - Click on the platform to view the name and coordinates.
    - The oil platform markers can be toggled on and off in the "Stations and Oil Platforms" section of the Control Center.

Control Center

  • Timestep

    - To change the hour that the map is showing surface currents, simply press the "-1 hour" or "+1 hour" buttons.
    - This will alter the UTC time is the display, and can be reset from pressing the "+1 day" until it defaults to the current UTC time.

  • RTV Products

    - The timeframe in which the surface currents are measured can be modified with the "Averaged" (average u-v components over 25 hours) or the "Hourly" options.
    - Resolution can be adjusted with the dropdown menu (500m, 1km, 2km, 6km).
    - Note that most HF Radars have a 6km or 2km resolution, and the lower resolutions are only applicable in a few areas.

  • Color Scheme

    - The "Color Scheme" can be toggled to change the color of the map vectors.
    - The range can also be altered to increase variability in colors of current strengths across the map.
    - Changes in the unit dropdown will also change the units in the u-v components on the map.

  • Coordinate Locator

    - Enter in a pair of coordinates (-90 to 90 latitude, -180 to 180 longitude) to view the screen at this location.
    - The "Submit" button must be clicked in order to reflect changes.
    - If you get lost, you can also use the "Back Home" button to navigate back to the Southern California coast :)

  • Stations and Oil Platforms

    - To toggle the Station and Oil Platform markers on the map, simply check or uncheck the boxes in this section.
    - The station markers display the real-time status of each HF Radar, as well as a link with more in-depth analysis of the Station Diagnostics.
